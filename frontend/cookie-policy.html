1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you're visiting
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by a domain other than the one you're visiting (e.g., advertising networks)

5. How to Control Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Clear cookies when closing your browser
  • Receive notifications before cookies are set

To manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site permissions

5.2 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific types of cookies:

  • Google Ads: Google Ads Settings
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: DAA Opt-Out
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance: EDAA Opt-Out

5.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals websites that you don't want your online activity tracked. However, there is no industry standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, you may experience:

  • Inability to log in to your account
  • Loss of personalized settings and preferences
  • Reduced website functionality
  • Repeated display of cookie consent banners
  • Less relevant advertisements

Important: Essential cookies are necessary for our website to function and cannot be disabled through our cookie settings. However, you can block them through your browser settings, though this may prevent you from using our services.
